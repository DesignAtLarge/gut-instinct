1. The human microbiome:
A revolution in our understanding of human health

Your body has roughly 38 trillion bacterial cells. The entire genomic collection of all these microbes makes up your very own human microbiome.

There are surprising links between our health and our microbe buddies! For example, rheumatoid arthritis patients have altered gut and oral bacteria [1]. Similarly, Crohn’s disease patients have been found with signficantly reduced microbial diversities [2].

So how might changing the gut microbiome of such patients influence their symptoms? 🤔

We are at the tip of an iceberg of scientific discoveries! However, the scientific community still needs to know much more...


1. The oral and gut microbiomes are perturbed in rheumatoid arthritis and partly normalized after treatment. Xuan Zhang, Dongya Zhang, and Huijue et al. Jia. 2015. Nat Med 21, 8: 895–905.

2. The human microbiome: at the interface of health and disease. I. Cho and M.J. Blaser. 2012. Nature Reviews Genetics 13, 4: 260–270.

2. Deciphering the microbiome requires ideas and information from you!

Governments have invested millions into understanding microbiome and their effects all around us! [1]

The American Gut Project has already identified millions of microbes in thousands of peoples' gut - Firmicutes, Bacteroidetes, you name it! However, researchers now need to understand what affects the microbes in our guts. How are our gut microbial systems changing?

Where do we even begin to understand the human microbiome? If only there was an expert who already knew in great detail what affects your health...

3. It's You! Why You Should Become a Gut Scientist?

You are the one with the most knowledge about yourself! Maybe you never get sick on flights or road-trips? Or maybe some medicines never work on you! Using Gut Instinct, you can share insights about yourself with other Gut Scientists by answering and creating questions. Researchers will then use your insights to analyze existing data. And together we can make new discoveries!

Don't think it'll work? Here's one of many examples! 23andMe is a genetic testing site and online service that includes a discussion board [2]. On this forum, a user reported disliking the sounds of others eating. She wasn't alone; a 23andMe survey found 16,000 users with the same condition and a predictive genetic similarity among them!

Your contributions can lead to major scientific discoveries! Share your ideas using Gut Instinct - and hear back from people around the world and our experts!
